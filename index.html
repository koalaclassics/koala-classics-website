The Process

From "I want one" to in your garage

Seven clear steps. You stay in control of the money and the decision the whole way through — we handle the hard part across the Pacific.

STEP 01

Tell us what you want

The car, the era, the budget. We listen first.

STEP 02

We source options

We search auctions, dealers, and private sellers across Australia to find the right vehicle. Koala Classics fee: $3,000 total ($1,000 non-refundable deposit due upfront for 6 months of sourcing).

STEP 03

Connect with the seller

You negotiate directly with the seller. No hidden markup or middleman fees.

STEP 04

Independent inspection

An independent inspector examines the car in Australia. You arrange this inspection and pay $400 directly to the inspector. You receive the full report and decide whether to proceed — or walk away free with no further obligation.

STEP 05

Secure payment process

You fund an independent escrow account (fee ~2.4–1.9%, paid by you). Seller ships once funds are confirmed. Once the car is confirmed loaded and in transit, we pay the remaining $2,000 Koala Classics fee and escrow releases payment to the seller. Your funds remain protected by marine insurance and the independent inspection you already approved.

STEP 06

Export & shipping

We coordinate de-registration and export paperwork in Australia. You arrange and pay for ocean freight (~$3,500–$5,000) and marine insurance (~1.5% of vehicle value) via the freight forwarder.

STEP 07

Lands in the USA

Vehicle arrives at the US port. A customs broker clears it (~$300 fee, paid by you). You pay 2.5% federal import duty to US Customs. You then register with your state DMV (~$200) to complete the process.

Common Questions

The things buyers ask first

How does the 25-year rule work?

The U.S. 25-year rule exempts vehicles 25 years or older from federal safety (NHTSA) and emissions (EPA) standards. Any vehicle manufactured in 2001 or earlier (as of 2026) is eligible. You complete the EPA 3520-1 and DOT HS-7 exemption forms at the port of entry — no physical modifications required under federal law. State registration rules vary by location, so always check your specific state's requirements before importing.

What's included in your Koala Classics fee?

Our $3,000 Koala Classics fee is structured as follows: $1,000 non-refundable deposit (due upfront) covers 6 months of professional sourcing, market research, vehicle searches across Australia, seller communications, and coordination. $2,000 (due when the car is confirmed loaded and in transit) covers de-registration, export coordination, escrow management, and ongoing support. All other costs—vehicle price, inspection, shipping, duties, insurance, and customs—are paid separately to their respective providers.

Can I negotiate the price directly with the seller?

Yes. You negotiate directly with the Australian seller — we introduce you and facilitate the conversation, but you stay in complete control of pricing and terms. No hidden markups or middleman fees. You negotiate a fair price with the seller, then we help you proceed safely through an independent, escrow-protected process.

What happens after my car arrives in the USA?

Once your car lands in the USA, you'll need to:
• Register it with your state's DMV
• Obtain insurance (required before registration in most states)
• Understand state-specific requirements (vary by state — some states have additional inspection or documentation needs)

We provide all necessary import documentation and can answer questions about the process. For specific questions about your vehicle or state, email adminkoalaclassics@gmail.com and we'll provide guidance and answer your questions.

Do you help with registration or DMV paperwork after arrival?

No — you handle registration and DMV paperwork directly with your state. However, we provide all necessary documentation from the import (bill of lading, import forms, inspection reports) and we're happy to answer any questions about the process. If you need clarification on any paperwork or have state-specific questions, contact adminkoalaclassics@gmail.com and we'll help where we can.

How long does the entire process take?

Typically 2–4 months from start to delivery, depending on:
• Vehicle availability (sourcing the right car)
• Inspection coordination
• Shipping schedules (varies by season)
• Customs clearance at US port

Once your car arrives and you verify its condition, payment is released to the seller. We keep you updated at every stage and provide regular progress updates.

Is my payment protected if something goes wrong?

Yes. Your funds are held by an independent escrow service — not by Koala Classics. Escrow holds your money safely until you inspect the vehicle, verify its condition and documentation, and approve delivery. Only then is payment released to the seller. You stay in complete control of when funds are released. If the vehicle doesn't meet expectations, you can refuse to release payment.

Who pays for the independent inspection and how do I arrange it?

You arrange the independent inspection and pay $400 directly to the inspector. Once we've found a candidate car you're interested in, we'll coordinate timing and details. You receive the full inspection report and can walk away without further obligation if it doesn't meet your expectations. The $400 inspection fee is non-refundable (the work has been completed), but it's a small investment for certainty before you commit to purchasing.

What is marine insurance and is it mandatory?

Marine insurance protects your vehicle's full value during ocean transport. Standard carrier liability does not cover total loss — if the car is damaged or lost at sea, you absorb the cost without insurance. We strongly recommend (and consider essential) arranging marine insurance (~1.5% of vehicle value) through your freight forwarder before the car ships. The cost is shown in the cost calculator.

What if the inspection report shows problems with the car?

It's your decision. You can walk away from the deal with no further obligation beyond the $400 inspection fee (the work was completed). If the vehicle doesn't meet expectations, we'll source another candidate at no additional deposit — your $1,000 deposit covers up to 6 months of sourcing attempts. Alternatively, you can negotiate the price down with the seller if minor issues are discovered.

Who initiates the escrow and how does it work?

You initiate the escrow once you and the seller have agreed on the vehicle and price. We'll guide you through the process. Here's the sequence: (1) You fund the independent escrow account and pay the escrow fee (~2.4–1.9% of vehicle price). (2) Escrow confirms funds are held safely. (3) Seller ships the vehicle. (4) Once the car is confirmed loaded (bill of lading issued), we notify you and pay the $2,000 Koala Classics shipment fee — escrow then releases your purchase money to the seller. (5) Vehicle arrives at the US port. (6) You inspect it and proceed with customs clearance. Koala Classics never touches the purchase money — it's held entirely by an independent escrow service.

What if the seller backs out after I've committed?

If the seller withdraws after you've funded escrow but before the car ships, your purchase money remains in escrow and is returned to you. You'll lose the $1,000 deposit (it was work completed during sourcing), but your purchase funds are protected. We'll offer to source another vehicle within the remainder of your 6-month sourcing period at no additional deposit.

What if the car arrives damaged or different from the inspection report?

You have protection through the marine insurance you purchased (which we recommend as mandatory) and the independent inspection you approved before shipment. If the car's condition differs significantly from the inspection report, your marine insurance covers damage from transit. This is why we emphasize the importance of thorough pre-shipment inspection and marine insurance — they protect you if something goes wrong.

Who pays for customs clearance and import broker fees?

A customs broker handles the paperwork to clear your vehicle at the US port of entry. You pay the customs broker fee (~$300). You also pay the federal import duty (2.5% of the vehicle's value) to US Customs at the time of entry. Both costs are shown in the cost calculator.

What if my state has stricter import rules than the federal 25-year exemption?

The federal 25-year rule exempts eligible vehicles from NHTSA safety and EPA emissions standards. However, individual US states may have additional requirements — some require extra inspections, documentation, or modifications. Before importing, verify your state's specific rules directly with your state's DMV. We can provide general guidance, but confirming your state's requirements is your responsibility. We recommend researching your state's rules early, in Step 1 of the process.

What if I find a car myself? Do I still pay the $1,000 deposit?

If you've already found a car and just need Koala Classics to coordinate the export, inspection, and logistics, you pay $0 deposit and only the $2,000 coordination fee once the car is in transit. The $1,000 deposit applies only when Koala sources the vehicle for you.
