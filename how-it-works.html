How It Works

A complete breakdown of the Koala Classics process: 7 steps from sourcing to delivery, transparent fees, and everything you need to know.

Seven Clear Steps

From initial inquiry to final delivery. You stay in control of the money and decision-making the entire way.

STEP 01

Tell us what you want

The car, the era, the budget. We listen first. This is where we understand your dream vehicle and confirm you're eligible to import it.

STEP 02

We source options

We search auctions, dealers, and private sellers across Australia to find the right vehicle. Koala Classics fee: $3,000 total ($1,000 non-refundable deposit due upfront for 6 months of sourcing).

STEP 03

Connect with the seller

You negotiate directly with the seller. No hidden markup or middleman fees. You stay in complete control of pricing and terms.

STEP 04

Independent inspection

An independent inspector examines the car in Australia. You arrange this specialized export inspection and pay $400 directly to the inspector. (This is higher than a standard local check because it includes asbestos verification, USDA biosecurity clearance, 25-year eligibility confirmation, and a comprehensive photographic dossier for customs — protecting you from thousands in port fees or confiscation.) You receive the full report and decide whether to proceed — or walk away free with no further obligation.

STEP 05

Secure payment process

You fund an independent escrow account (fee ~2.4–1.9%, paid by you). Seller ships once funds are confirmed. Once the car is confirmed loaded and in transit, we pay the remaining $2,000 Koala Classics fee and escrow releases payment to the seller. Your funds remain protected by marine insurance and the independent inspection you already approved.

STEP 06

Export & shipping

We coordinate de-registration and export paperwork in Australia. You arrange and pay for ocean freight (~$3,500–$5,000) and marine insurance (~1.5% of vehicle value) via the freight forwarder.

STEP 07

Lands in the USA

Vehicle arrives at the US port. A customs broker clears it (~$300 fee, paid by you). You pay 2.5% federal import duty to US Customs. You then register with your state DMV (~$200) to complete the process.

Frequently Asked Questions

How does the 25-year rule work?

The U.S. 25-year rule exempts vehicles 25 years or older from federal safety (NHTSA) and emissions (EPA) standards. Any vehicle manufactured in 2001 or earlier (as of 2026) is eligible. You complete the EPA 3520-1 and DOT HS-7 exemption forms at the port of entry — no physical modifications required under federal law. State registration rules vary by location, so always check your specific state's requirements before importing.

All other costs—vehicle price, inspection, shipping, duties, insurance, and customs—are paid separately to their respective providers.

Why does the $400 inspection cost more than a local Australian check?

A standard Australian pre-purchase inspection typically costs $150–$300. But exporting a classic car to the USA requires specialized verification that a regular mechanic doesn't perform. Here's why the $400 fee is industry-standard and protects you: Asbestos verification — Both Australian Border Force and US Customs have zero-tolerance policies on asbestos in imported vehicles. A standard mechanic checks if brakes work; an export inspector verifies that vintage brake linings, clutch plates, and gaskets do not contain asbestos. If the car arrives at a US port and fails asbestos inspection, you face thousands in containment, testing, and cleaning fees — or even confiscation. USDA biosecurity clearance — The US Department of Agriculture requires imported vehicles to be completely free of foreign soil, pests, seeds, and plant material. The inspector checks the undercarriage, wheel arches, engine bay, and cavities through a biosecurity lens. If biosecurity issues are found, the vehicle requires professional steam-cleaning ($400–$800) before it can clear customs. 25-year eligibility confirmation — To legally import without federal safety standards, the vehicle must be at least 25 years old down to the exact month. An export inspector manually verifies the chassis number, VIN plate, and engine stamps match official paperwork, and checks for hidden structural rust or unrecorded repairs that could cause rejection at the port. Customs documentation dossier — Export inspectors provide a comprehensive photographic record (far more detailed than a standard local inspection) that your customs broker needs to prove the car's condition before shipping and establish a baseline for any transit damage claims. Bottom line: A $400 export inspection is cheap insurance. It prevents you from absorbing $100–$200+ per day in port storage fees, $400–$800 in biosecurity cleaning penalties, or confiscation at arrival. This is why specialist export inspectors charge a premium.

What is marine insurance and is it mandatory?

Marine insurance protects your vehicle's full value during ocean transport. Standard carrier liability does not cover total loss — if the car is damaged or lost at sea, you absorb the cost without insurance. We strongly recommend (and consider essential) arranging marine insurance (~1.5% of vehicle value) through your freight forwarder before the car ships. The cost is shown in the cost calculator.

We recommend researching your state's rules early, in Step 1 of the process.
